Threads Across the Instruments

  • Chosen vs inherited — the distinction every instrument returns to. What did you choose, and what arrived before you had the language to refuse it?
  • The gap between inner and outer — SELF v SELFIE, When Yes Means No, and Social Contract all examine the space between what you perform and what you feel.
  • Systems and individuals — RPG4LIFE, BIRTHMARKS, and Social Contract ask how structures shape the options available before any individual choice is made.
  • The watching eye — Surveillance and Input/Output examine how being observed — by institutions, algorithms, other people — changes what you do and who you become.
  • Pressure and position — The Danger Room and When Yes Means No test what happens to your stated beliefs when the cost of holding them increases.

Recommended Sequence

  • Start with Who Are You, Really — it establishes the central question and sets the reflective register for everything that follows.
  • Pair Default Settings + BIRTHMARKS — inherited beliefs alongside inherited identity. The internal and the external version of the same problem.
  • Use RPG4LIFE + Social Contract together — both examine how assigned roles shape what choices feel available.
  • Place The Danger Room in the middle or late in the sequence — it requires enough self-knowledge to make the pressure felt.
  • End with Surveillance or Input/Output — both ask what you want to do with what you now know. The outward turn after the inward work.
Abitur Essay Questions — The Individual and Society
To what extent is individual identity shaped by social structures rather than personal choice?
How does the expectation of surveillance change individual behaviour — and what does this tell us about the relationship between freedom and observation?
Is conformity always a form of weakness, or can it be a rational response to social pressure? Where do you draw the line?
What is the difference between a self that is constructed and a self that is authentic? Is the distinction meaningful?
To what extent can consent be considered genuine when it is given under conditions of unequal power?
